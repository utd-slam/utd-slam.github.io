Alzheimer's and Music Study

We are enrolling patients with Alzheimer’s Disease for a study investigating the long-term clinical efficacy of a music-based intervention called SoRhythm on slowing the progression of Alzheimer’s Disease symptoms including memory and language impairment. Those who participate will be asked to come in person to the Callier Center Richardson for two sessions which take 2 hours each. In these sessions, those who participate will receive cognitive assessments, a hearing test, and will listen to SoRhythm while receiving a brain recording. After all of the in person sessions are complete, those who participate will be compensated for their time. Efficacy of a Novel Sound Therapy on Language Functioning in Patients with Alzheimer's Disease: A Clinical Trial

We are enrolling patients with Alzheimer’s Disease for a 2 month clinical trial investigating the long-term efficacy of a music-based intervention called SoRhythm on slowing the progression of Alzheimer’s Disease symptoms including memory and language impairment. Those who participate will be asked to come in person to the Callier Center Richardson a total of four times for cognitive assessments and an EEG recording. In between these in person sessions, participants will be asked to listen to SoRhythm everyday for 2 months in the comfort of their own home. Those who participate will be compensated for their time. Efficacy of a Novel Sound Therapy on Language Functioning for Promoting Healthy Aging Study

We are enrolling adults aged 40+ to participate in a study investigating the long-term clinical efficacy of a music-based intervention called SoRhythm on promoting healthy aging. Those who participate will be asked to come in person to the Callier Center Richardson for two sessions which take 2 hours each. In these sessions, those who participate will receive cognitive assessments, a hearing test, and will listen to SoRhythm while receiving a brain recording. After all of the in person sessions are complete, those who participate will be compensated for their time.
